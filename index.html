• Mabel Yao is a researcher specializing in Statistical Machine Learning, Statistical Inference, Computational Statistics, Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI), and Data Science. She earned a Ph.D. in Statistics and an M.S. in Computer Science from North Dakota State University. With an interdisciplinary background spanning statistics, computer science, data science, and engineering, she develops rigorous, interpretable, and data-driven methodologies for solving complex real-world problems.

• Beyond theoretical research and technological development, Mabel Yao is passionate about applying statistical and AI-driven methodologies to solve real-world problems. Her interests lie particularly at the intersection of Tourism Economics, Behavioral Economics, E-commerce Economics, and Pricing Science, where data-driven insights can inform decision-making and create practical impact. She has long been fascinated by psychology, philosophy, and economics—disciplines that seek to explain the fundamental principles governing human behavior and the world around us. This interdisciplinary curiosity naturally motivates her to integrate rigorous statistical theory, statistical machine learning, explainable AI, and data science with these fields.

• For Mabel Yao, one of the most rewarding aspects of research is using AI and Data Science grounded methods to uncover new insights into classical questions, offering quantitative perspectives that complement and enrich established theories. By bridging advanced analytical techniques with human-centered applications, she aims to develop research that is both scientifically rigorous and practically meaningful.
